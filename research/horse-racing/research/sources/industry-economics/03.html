1 ## The State of Thoroughbred Horse Racing in the United States: An Economic ## Analysis and the Path to Industry Stability The sport of thoroughbred horse racing was once the most popular sport in the United States. Although large events like the Kentucky Derby and the Belmont Stakes still capture popular interest, the sport has on most days been reduced to a niche market, albeit with a highly interested core audience. In this research paper, we study recent macroeconomic trends as well as microeco nomic decisions that have shaped the sport’s economic structure. The paper’s primary objective is to take a snapshot of the current state of the sport based on 2022 data, and to provide analysis by comparing this data with the same data from the past 20 ye ars, beginning in 2003. By examining these data points and applying simple economic principles (e.g., supply and demand, perfect competition vs . oligopoly/monopolistic markets), we aim to identify and understand the sport’s prevailing secular trends and gain insights into its current economic dynamic. At the heart of our examination is rooting all of our findings and narratives in as much data as possible, leveraging access to an extensive database of information that has remained largely unexplored until now. Surprisingly, despite the sport's rich hist ory and widespread popularity, this kind of comprehensive economic analysis has rarely been attempted. Limited access to this data and the fractured nature of stakeholder ownership and involvement within the sport, namely racetrack operators and thoroughbr ed owners, are two of the main reasons why the state of the sport has so rarely been assessed and altered. We’d like to hope that the information provided within may lead the sport to support more rational decision -making. With that in mind, a quick preview of our findings would suggest: 1.
