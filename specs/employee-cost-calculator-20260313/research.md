# Employee Cost Calculator - Research

## Problem
Employers need to understand the **true cost** of hiring an employee beyond just the base salary. The total cost includes payroll taxes, benefits, insurance, equipment, office space, and other overhead. This is critical for:
- Budget planning and headcount decisions
- Freelancer vs. employee cost comparison
- Understanding cost-per-hour for billing/project planning

## Key Cost Components

### Mandatory Payroll Taxes (US-centric defaults, configurable)
1. **Social Security (FICA OASDI)**: 6.2% of wages up to $168,600 (2024 wage base)
2. **Medicare (FICA HI)**: 1.45% of all wages (no cap)
3. **Federal Unemployment (FUTA)**: 6.0% on first $7,000, but effective rate ~0.6% after state credit
4. **State Unemployment (SUTA)**: Varies by state, typically 1-5% on first $7,000-$56,500

### Common Benefits
- Health insurance: employer portion, typically $6,000-$15,000/year
- Dental/vision: $500-$2,000/year
- 401(k) match: typically 3-6% of salary
- PTO cost: paid days off represent salary cost for non-productive time
- Workers' compensation: 0.5-3% of payroll

### Overhead Costs
- Equipment (computer, phone, software licenses): $2,000-$5,000/year
- Office space per employee: $3,000-$12,000/year
- Training/professional development: $500-$2,000/year
- Other overhead (HR, accounting, misc): variable

## Key Metrics to Output
1. **Total Annual Cost**: salary + all taxes + benefits + overhead
2. **Monthly Cost**: total / 12
3. **Effective Hourly Cost**: total / working hours per year
4. **Overhead Multiplier**: total cost / base salary (typically 1.25x - 1.5x)
5. **Tax Burden**: total employer taxes
6. **Benefits Cost**: total benefits
7. **Cost Breakdown %**: percentage of each category
