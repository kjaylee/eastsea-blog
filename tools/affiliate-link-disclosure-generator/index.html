Frequently Asked Questions
Where should I place my affiliate disclosure?
FTC guidelines require disclosure to be "clear and conspicuous." For blogs: above the first affiliate link. For YouTube: in the description box, spoken at the start of the video, and in the video title or first 15 seconds. For Instagram: in the caption before the first link, or in the bio with a link to a disclosure page.
Do #ad and #affiliate hashtags satisfy FTC requirements?
On Instagram and TikTok, #ad or #affiliate in the caption is generally accepted as sufficient disclosure IF it appears early and is clearly visible. However, on YouTube, a verbal disclosure at the start of the video is required in addition to any text disclosure. Never bury disclosures in "full disclosure" pages that users have to click to reach.
What happens if I don't have an affiliate disclosure?
FTC violations can result in fines of $50,120 per violation (as of 2024). The ASA in the UK can require you to remove content and ban future advertising. In most cases, affiliates receive a warning first — but repeat violations or deceptive practices can trigger formal action. The risk is real and growing as affiliate marketing scrutiny increases.
Do I need a disclosure if I'm using a discount code?
Yes. If you receive any benefit — commission, discount, free product, or reciprocal arrangement — you must disclose it. A discount code you share that was provided by a brand for affiliate purposes absolutely requires disclosure.
