❓ FAQ — Circle.so Fees Explained
What are Circle.so paywall transaction fees?
Circle charges a platform transaction fee on every paywall sale: 2% on the Professional plan, 1% on Business, and 0.5% on Circle Plus. These are separate from Stripe payment processing fees, which are also charged on every transaction.
What is the Professional vs Business break-even GMV?
The standard break-even is $11,000/month in GMV. Above that threshold, the Business plan's lower transaction fee (1% vs 2%) saves more than the $110/month premium over Professional. The formula: ($199 − $89) ÷ (2% − 1%) = $11,000.
Why does subscription cost more than one-time payment?
Stripe Billing adds an extra 0.7% on top of the base 2.9% + $0.30 fee for recurring/subscription charges. One-time invoiced payments may incur a smaller 0.4% invoicing add-on. This makes subscriptions ~0.3% more expensive per transaction compared to one-time sales at the same price.
What is Circle Plus and how do I model it?
Circle Plus is a custom enterprise pricing tier with a 0.5% transaction fee — the lowest of any plan. Because the monthly platform fee is negotiated per account, this calculator requires you to enter your quoted monthly price manually before showing Circle Plus results and break-even comparisons.
Are Stripe fees separate from Circle fees?
Yes. Circle's transaction fee goes to Circle. Stripe's payment processing fee (2.9% + $0.30 by default, plus Billing add-ons for subscriptions) goes to Stripe. You pay both on every transaction. This calculator combines them so you see your true net payout in one view.
Do these fees apply internationally?
Circle's paywall fees are the same globally, but Stripe charges different rates for non-US cards (typically +1.5% for international cards). This calculator defaults to US domestic rates. Check Stripe's pricing page for your country's rates and adjust the advanced fields accordingly.
