❓ 자주 묻는 질문 (FAQ)
How much house can I afford on a $100K salary?
With a $100K salary, no other debts, 6.5% rate, and 20% down, you can typically afford a home around $350K–$420K using the standard 28/36 DTI rule. Your actual number depends on property taxes, insurance, and interest rates in your area.
What is the 28/36 rule?
The 28/36 rule says your total housing costs should not exceed 28% of gross monthly income (front-end ratio), and total debt payments including housing should not exceed 36% (back-end ratio). FHA loans allow 31/43, and VA loans use only a 41% back-end limit.
How does FHA differ from conventional for affordability?
FHA loans allow higher DTI ratios (31% front-end, 43% back-end vs 28/36 for conventional) and require only 3.5% down payment. However, FHA requires mortgage insurance premium (MIP) at 0.85% regardless of down payment size, which adds to monthly costs.
Does PMI affect how much house I can afford?
Yes. PMI (Private Mortgage Insurance) is required when your down payment is less than 20%. The PMI cost reduces the amount available for principal & interest, lowering your maximum affordable price by roughly 5–8%.
Should I put 20% down?
Putting 20% down eliminates PMI and reduces your monthly payment, but it isn't always necessary. If saving for 20% significantly delays your purchase, the opportunity cost of waiting (rising prices, rent paid) may outweigh PMI savings. Run the numbers for your situation.
