About Ohm's Law

Ohm's Law states that the current through a conductor between two points is directly proportional to the voltage across the two points and inversely proportional to the resistance. It is written as V = I × R.

When combined with the power formula P = V × I, you get a complete set of 12 interrelated formulas. Knowing any two values — voltage (V), current (I), resistance (R), or power (P) — lets you calculate all four.

This calculator is useful for LED current-limiting resistor calculations, estimating power consumption of DC circuits, sizing wire gauges, troubleshooting electronic components, and learning basic electronics.
