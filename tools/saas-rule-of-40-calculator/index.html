Formula basis

Rule of 40 = ARR growth rate % + profit margin %. This page stays narrow on that equation rather than blending in quick ratio, burn multiple, or magic number logic.

When to use it

Use the same period basis for ARR inputs, and enter the profit margin definition your team already uses internally. Consistency matters more than fancy inputs here.

Enter the same period for both ARR fields. Profit margin can be negative.

Interpretation

Rule of 40 is a heuristic, not GAAP. Use it as an operator scorecard, not a substitute for full financial analysis.

Summary

FAQ

What is the SaaS Rule of 40?

It is a simple benchmark that adds growth rate and profit margin into one score. A company growing 30% with a 12% profit margin has a Rule of 40 score of 42.

Should I use ARR or revenue?

Either can work if you stay consistent. This page is ARR-first because that fits the surrounding SaaS KPI tools in this repo, but the formula logic is about percentage growth plus margin.

Which margin definition should I use?

Use the one your team actually reports internally and keep it consistent. v1 intentionally avoids a selector for EBITDA, EBIT, or free cash flow margin.

Is 40 always the right target?

No. It is a common heuristic, not a law. Early-stage companies may run below it for a while, and mature efficient businesses may comfortably exceed it.
