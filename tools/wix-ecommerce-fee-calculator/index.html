Wix eCommerce Fees Explained

Wix charges fees in two layers: a Wix Payments processing fee on every transaction, and — only on the Free plan — an additional Wix transaction fee. Paid plans (Core and above) eliminate the extra transaction fee entirely.

Wix Plan Fee Rates (2024)

  • Free ($0/mo): 2.9% + $0.30 Wix Payments processing + 2.9% Wix transaction fee
  • Core (~$29/mo): 2.9% + $0.30 processing only — no extra transaction fee
  • Business (~$36/mo): 2.6% + $0.30 processing — lower rate, no transaction fee
  • Business Elite (~$159/mo): 2.3% + $0.30 processing — lowest rate, no transaction fee

When Should You Upgrade from Free to Core?

The Free plan's extra 2.9% transaction fee adds up quickly. At just $1,000/month GMV, you're paying an extra $29 in transaction fees — roughly the cost of the Core plan. For any store doing consistent sales, Core pays for itself almost immediately.

Wix Payments vs Third-Party Processors

You can use third-party processors (Stripe, PayPal, Square) with Wix, but the Wix transaction fee still applies on Free plan regardless of payment processor. On paid plans, only the processor's fee applies — Wix charges nothing extra.

FAQ

Does Wix charge a transaction fee on all plans?
No. Only the Free plan has an additional Wix transaction fee (2.9%). Core, Business, and Business Elite plans have no Wix transaction fee — you only pay Wix Payments processing rates.
What is the Wix Payments processing fee?
Wix Payments processing fees vary by plan: Free and Core pay 2.9% + $0.30, Business pays 2.6% + $0.30, and Business Elite pays 2.3% + $0.30 per transaction.
How do I calculate the price to charge to receive a specific net amount?
Use the "Net Target → Sale Price" reverse calculator above. Enter the amount you want to keep, and the calculator will show the exact price you need to charge your customers to cover all Wix fees.
Is Wix Payments available everywhere?
Wix Payments is available in select countries including the US, UK, Canada, Australia, and most of Europe. If unavailable in your region, you'll use a third-party processor, but the plan's transaction fee structure still applies.
